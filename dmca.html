Upholding these rights is a fundamental part of our mission to provide a fair and trustworthy gaming environment. We are firmly committed to full compliance with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) and other relevant copyright laws and regulations worldwide.

When notified of an alleged infringement, we act quickly and responsibly to investigate and address the matter. This includes taking appropriate steps such as removing or disabling access to any content found to infringe on copyrights on our platform, in accordance with legal requirements and best practices. We strive to balance the protection of rights holders with the rights of users to a fair process.

How to Report Copyright Infringement

If you believe that material hosted on drift-hunters-game.github.io violates your copyright, we encourage you to notify us immediately so we can resolve the issue as promptly as possible.

To submit a valid copyright infringement claim, please provide a written notice containing the following specific details:

  • Your full legal name, physical mailing address, telephone number, and an active email address where you can be reached.

  • A detailed description of the copyrighted work(s) you claim have been infringed upon, including registration numbers or other relevant documentation if available.

  • The exact URL(s) on drift-hunters-game.github.io where the allegedly infringing content resides.

  • A statement, made under penalty of perjury, affirming that you have a good faith belief that the use of the content in question is unauthorized by you, your agent, or the law.

  • A declaration that the information provided in the notice is accurate and that you are authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner.

  • Your physical or electronic signature to authenticate the notice.

Please send your infringement notice directly to our designated DMCA agent using the contact details listed below. Incomplete or improperly formatted notifications may delay the processing of your claim.

Counter-Notification Procedure

If your content has been removed or disabled due to a DMCA complaint and you believe this was a mistake or due to misidentification, you have the right to submit a counter-notice.

A valid counter-notice must include:

  • Your full name, current mailing address, telephone number, and email address.

  • Identification of the material that was removed or disabled, including the URL(s) where the content appeared before removal.

  • A statement, under penalty of perjury, that you have a good faith belief that the material was mistakenly removed or disabled.

  • Consent to the jurisdiction of the federal district court in your jurisdiction (or another appropriate court), and an agreement to accept service of process from the original claimant.

  • Your handwritten or electronic signature.

Upon receiving a proper counter-notice, we will promptly forward it to the original complainant. If the complainant does not file a lawsuit within the timeframe prescribed by law, we will restore the removed material as required.

Protecting copyrights is a critical priority for us. We take repeated violations seriously and maintain strict policies in accordance with the DMCA and applicable laws. Users or accounts found to be repeatedly infringing copyrights may face suspension, termination, or permanent bans from accessing drift-hunters-game.github.io.

This approach helps us maintain a healthy, lawful environment for all users and respects the rights of content creators.
